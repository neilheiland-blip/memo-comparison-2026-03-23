Contemporary Art Spotlight

Shahzia Sikander and the M+ Façade Commission

Shahzia Sikander's "3 to 12 Nautical Miles" launches on M+ Museum façade March 23, 2026

Pakistani-American artist Shahzia Sikander (born 1969) launches her co-commissioned video work "3 to 12 Nautical Miles" on the M+ Museum façade today, March 23, providing Hong Kong Art Week with a museum-grade anchor alongside commercial programming.

Sikander earned her BFA from the National College of Arts, Lahore (1992) and MFA from Rhode Island School of Design (1995), where she pioneered contemporary approaches to traditional Indo-Persian miniature painting. Her work bridges Eastern and Western artistic traditions through digital media, animation, and large-scale installations.

The M+ commission matters because it demonstrates how major museums coordinate institutional programming with commercial art fair calendars. Rather than competing with Art Basel Hong Kong's March 27-29 run, M+ provides complementary cultural programming that elevates the entire week's cultural significance.

Market context: Sikander's work appears regularly at auction with pieces selling in the $50,000-$200,000 range. Her institutional presence includes Whitney Biennial (2000), Venice Biennale (2001), and permanent collections at MoMA, Guggenheim, and Tate Modern. The Fukuoka Asian Art Museum acquired her video installations in 2019.

The timing reveals Hong Kong's strategy to position itself as more than a commercial art market — it's building institutional cultural infrastructure that rivals established art capitals. The M+ facade commission runs concurrent with Lee Bul's survey exhibition, creating a museum-led counter-narrative to pure commercial fair activity.

Technical innovation: "3 to 12 Nautical Miles" employs Sikander's signature approach of layering traditional miniature painting techniques with contemporary digital animation. The work references maritime boundaries while exploring sovereignty and cultural navigation.
